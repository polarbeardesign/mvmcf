Working Together, Making a Difference
in the Health of Our Community

As a not-for-profit corporation, the Madison Valley Medical Center Foundation (MVMCF) exists solely to support the mission of the Medical Center and the programs and services it provides.

The Foundation accepts contributions from individuals, corporations, foundations, and other donors for facility improvement, equipment purchases and health programs designed to enhance the quality of life for residents of Madison County and southwestern Montana.

The Foundation is very fortunate to have the support of community members, especially the members of the Medical Center Auxiliary Association. These dedicated men and women work tirelessly to help raise funds for the Medical Center as well as serve as the Medical Center’s “ambassadors” out in the community.

Your Support is Key

Your support is still needed to complete many aspects of the facility, such as; fulfilling department "needs lists" for various types of much needed medical equipment, installing permanent signage inside and outside, seeding grass and installing landscaping.

We encourage the Madison Valley community to stop in, tour the facility, and meet the medical providers and the caring staff. For more information on how you can help support the Madison Valley Medical Center, contact the Foundation Executive Director, Christie S. Trapp at (406) 682-6630 or 6641.

The Medical Center has
four Board Certified Providers on Staff

The Medical Center has four board certified providers on staff. R.D. Marks, MD is the Chief of medical staff. The other providers are; Dr. Curtis Blake, Dr. Patricia Moran, and PA-C Marty Hensel. You can support the Medical Center by making an appointment to have your medical needs met locally.  Call the hospital at (406) 682-6862 or the clinic at (406) 682-4223.

The Madison Valley Medical Center

The beautiful new facility is filled with State-of-the-Art Technology. Patients have the comfort of private rooms and private baths, most rooms also enjoy beautiful views of the valley and surrounding mountains.
The building is a two-level, 36,470 square foot medical facility with ten acute care hospital beds, a full laboratory, imaging department, emergency room and a nine exam room clinic.
